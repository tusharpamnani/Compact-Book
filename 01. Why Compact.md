# Why Compact?

This document explores why Compact exists and how it compares to alternatives.

> Source: [what-is-midnight](https://docs.midnight.network/what-is-midnight), [compact](https://docs.midnight.network/compact)

## Landscape

| Approach | Privacy | Accessibility |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| Raw ZK circuits | Full | Requires cryptographic expertise |
| Traditional smart contracts (Solidity) | None | Accessible to developers |
| Compact | Privacy-preserving | TypeScript-like syntax |

## Key Factors

### 1. Accessibility

Traditional smart contracts are accessible but offer no privacy. Raw ZK development requires deep cryptographic expertise. Compact makes "privacy-preserving smart contracts accessible to mainstream developers", you write TypeScript-like syntax, the compiler produces proving circuits.

### 2. Privacy Model

Compact maintains two parallel states:
- **Public ledger**: on-chain, visible to all
- **Private state**: stored locally, never exposed

Computations happen locally on private data. A ZK proof of correctness is submitted on-chain. Validators verify the proof without seeing the inputs.

### 3. Type Safety

| Language | Type Safety |
|----------|-------------|
| JavaScript/Solidity | Dynamically or loosely typed |
| Compact | Strongly and statically typed, compiler rejects any program that doesn't type-check |

### 4. Verifiability vs. Privacy

You can have verifiability (everything on-chain) at the cost of privacy. Or privacy with raw circuits that are extremely difficult to write and audit. Compact gives you both without writing a single circuit by hand.

### 5. Compliance and Selective Disclosure

Compact supports selective disclosure, users choose exactly what to reveal and to whom. This enables regulated use cases (healthcare, finance, identity) without exposing underlying data.

## When to Choose Compact

- Need privacy-preserving logic on a public blockchain
- Want to prove correctness without revealing inputs
- TypeScript developer who doesn't want to learn ZK circuit design
- Regulated data or selective disclosure requirements

## When Not to Choose Compact

- No privacy requirements, a traditional public contract may be simpler
- Need Turing-complete computation, Compact is intentionally bounded
- Need dynamically-sized data structures, everything has compile-time-known size

## The Core Tradeoff

Compact trades Turing-completeness and dynamic typing for automatic ZK circuit compilation, strong type safety, and a privacy model that works at the language level.
